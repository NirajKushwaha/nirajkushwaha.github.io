The Triangle of Madness

What if conflicts could be grouped into a few fundamental types? This study uses a data-driven approach to reveal three archetypal forms of armed conflict across Africa‚Äîwhile also showing why even rich data can fall short when predicting how severe conflicts will become.

Synchrotron emission from supernovas

High-energy cosmic particles reveal themselves through the light they emit while spiraling through magnetic fields. This project explores how synchrotron radiation changes when those fields are non-uniform, \ offering a more realistic window into how astrophysical sources like supernova remnants and nebulae shine.

Rippling Chimera in Oscillator Networks

Chimera states‚Äîwhere coherence and incoherence coexist‚Äîare among the most intriguing behaviors in complex systems. This work demonstrates how machine learning can reliably predict the delays needed to create these states, bridging theoretical dynamics and experimental design.

Armed Conflict Avalanches and Mesoscale

From single incidents to regional crises, conflicts form connected chains rather than isolated events. This work reveals a hidden scale at which these connections‚Äî unfolding as conflict avalanches‚Äîbecome visible, offering a new way to study, interpret, and eventually anticipate the dynamics of armed conflict.

Population Waves in Sessile Organisms

Life follows striking mathematical patterns, from metabolism to population size‚Äîbut real ecosystems are shaped by competition and limited resources. This work aims to show how accounting for these interactions reveals mechanisms behind population oscillations and ecosystem instability. (PROJECT CURRENTLY ON HIATUS)
